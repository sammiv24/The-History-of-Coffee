Ethiopia

The history of coffee can be traced back to the ancient forests of Ethiopia in the 15th century.

Legend says the goat herder Kaldi first discovered these beans when he noticed that after eating the berries from a certain tree, his goats became so energetic that they did not want to sleep at night.

Arabia

Coffee cultivation then reached the Arabian peninsula. There, coffee houses called qahveh khaneh became popular for all kinds of social activity.

Europe

The bean soon made its way to Europe and became a popular beverage across the continent.

However, this was not the initial reaction of most Europeans. They first reacted with fear and called the drink a “bitter invention of Satan.” Pope Clement VIII was forced to step in and ultimately gave the beverage his approval.

New World

In the mid-1660s, Dutch colonists brought coffee to the New World.

Tea continued to be the favored drink until after the Boston Tea Party in 1773. After this event, drinking coffee became a patriotic symbol and took off as the most popular American drink.

Today, the coffee industry is valued at $20 billion and continues to be the most popular hot beverage on the market.
In recent years, movements like Grass Roots Coffee and the Fair Trade Movement have arised in order to promote ethically sourced beans.
